vary (ukrainian  hungarian vri or mezvri) is a village in zakarpattia oblast (province) of western ukraine it is located around 17 kilometres (11 mi) southeast of berehove at the confluence of the rivers tisza and borzsova not far from the ukrainian-hungarian border administratively the village belongs to the berehove raion zakarpattia oblast historically the name originates from the hungarian word vr meaning castle the village was first mentioned as vri in 1320 and was previously known as borsovavra
== population ==
in 1921 the village had a population of 2,625 mostly hungarians as of 2012 the population includes 3,302 inhabitants of which 3008 are hungarians
